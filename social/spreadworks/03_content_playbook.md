# Spreadworks — Content Playbook (Project B)

How the Spreadworks avatar turns the tool + bots + community into a steady content
stream. Primary platforms: **X/Twitter**, **Discord/Reddit (community)**, and
**short-form video** (the tool demos beautifully), with **LinkedIn** for long-form.
Follow the compliance and CTA rules in `shared/`.

---

## Content pillars (rotate across these)

1. **Strategy education (the spine).** Demystify Double Diagonals, Double Calendars,
   Iron Butterflies, Iron Condors: payoff shapes, Greeks, P(profit), breakevens, and
   *how each one loses.* This is the wedge — calendars/diagonals are under-taught.
2. **GEX-Suggest demos.** Show the tool placing strikes at walls / around the flip
   point. "Build your spread around the dealers." Best short-form fuel.
3. **Community rhythm.** Repurpose the daily-brief engine: verse at open, a tip, an
   engagement prompt, a reflective close. Brings the Discord culture to the public feed.
4. **The four bots.** BREEZE/TIDE/DRIFT/FLOW as "four ways to play a range" — paper
   updates (labeled paper) and teaching how each shape behaves.
5. **Event & market reads.** Economic-event "typical behavior" intel (FOMC/CPI/NFP) —
   always framed as patterns, not predictions.

**Mix guide:** ~30% pillar 1, ~20% pillar 2, ~20% pillar 3, ~15% pillar 4, ~15% pillar
5. Education- and community-heavy; product mentions ride along.

## Post formats by platform

### X / Twitter (primary)
- **Strategy teardown thread:** one strategy, its payoff shape, when it wins, *how it
  loses*, and a GEX-aware strike example. End with soft CTA (tool/Discord in reply).
- **GEX-Suggest screenshot/gif:** "here's where the tool put my strikes today, and why."
- **"Defend your pick" prompt:** repurpose the engagement prompts to drive replies.
- **Daily tip:** one of the 155+ tips, in the founder's voice, standalone.
- **Myth-bust:** "Calendars aren't 'set and forget.' Here's the day vega turns on you."

### Discord (primary community home)
- The **daily brief** is the anchor: verse → market context + event intel → tip →
  engagement prompt → (EOD) reflection. The `/spread` command is the interactive hook.
- Host conversations, run the engagement prompts, celebrate members' disciplined trades,
  do live "let's build this spread" walk-throughs.

### Short-form video (YouTube Shorts / TikTok / Reels — strong fit here)
- **60-sec tool demos:** symbol in → GEX-Suggest → payoff diagram out. Visually clean,
  on-brand colors.
- **"What is a Double Calendar?" in 60 seconds**, with the payoff shape animating.
- **"Reading a payoff diagram"** micro-lessons.

### Reddit (secondary, careful)
- Value-first educational posts in r/options, r/thetagang. Follow each sub's self-promo
  rules; the tool/Discord link goes only where it genuinely helps.

### LinkedIn (secondary, long-form)
- Educational essays on spread construction and GEX; the "I built a GEX-powered spread
  lab and a community around discipline" founder narrative. Spell out acronyms.

## Repeatable series (lower the creative load)

- **"Spread of the Week"** — pick one strategy, full teardown + a GEX-aware setup.
- **"Built Around the Walls"** — GEX-Suggest demo on a chosen symbol.
- **"Four Currents"** — rotating spotlight on BREEZE / TIDE / DRIFT / FLOW.
- **"Open & Close"** — the verse-at-open / reflection-at-close rhythm, cross-posted.
- **"Typical, Not a Prediction"** — event-behavior explainers around the macro calendar.
- **"Payoff in 60"** — short-form "read this trade's shape" lessons.

## Hook bank (adapt, don't overuse verbatim)

- "Everyone trades Iron Condors. Almost nobody can explain a Double Calendar. Let's fix
  that."
- "I let the GEX map pick my strikes this morning. Here's where it put them — and why."
- "A payoff diagram tells you how a trade *loses* before you risk a dollar. Here's how
  to read one."
- "Four bots, four ways to play the same range. Which shape fits this week's tape?"
- "FOMC is Wednesday. Here's what SPY *typically* does around it — and why 'typically'
  is doing a lot of work in that sentence."
- "PUT = green, CALL = red in our app. Here's the logic, and why 'green = up' is a trap."

## Cadence (sustainable)

- **X:** 1–2/day (1 teach + 1 demo/tip), 1 teardown thread/week.
- **Discord:** the daily brief, every market day (it's largely automated — amplify it).
- **Short-form:** 2–3/week (the demos are repeatable and fast).
- **LinkedIn:** 1–2/week.
- **Reddit:** 1 genuinely useful post/week, max, where rules allow.
- **Anchor:** the daily Discord brief + a weekly "Spread of the Week."

---

## What a great Spreadworks post does (checklist)

- [ ] Teaches a strategy/GEX idea clearly, or hosts the community, or demos the tool.
- [ ] Shows the **loss case / risk**, not just the profit zone.
- [ ] Frames event/market reads as *typical, not predictions.*
- [ ] Names bots/strategies correctly (don't confuse with Iron Forge).
- [ ] Labels bot results as **paper**; treats the analyzer as a calculator, not a
      money machine.
- [ ] Follows the brand colors/typography in any graphic (PUT=green/CALL=red, mono
      numbers, no emoji in product chrome).
- [ ] CTA (if any) invites people to *learn/join the room*, points to `<SPREADWORKS_URL>`
      / `<DISCORD_INVITE>`, never to "profit."
