## 0. Scope & honest framing

Three sub-projects that turn SpreadWorks' directional dip-buyer into a small **vertical-spread**
suite, plus a cheap AI rationale on every entry:

- **A â€” UNDERTOW v2:** evolve the single long call into **directional debit verticals** â€” a
  **bull call spread** on dips, a **bear put spread** on overbought rollovers. Both directions,
  defined risk = net debit, cheaper entries than a naked long.
- **B â€” DELTA (new bot):** **directional credit spreads** â€” sell a **put credit spread** on the
  bullish (oversold-dip) setup, a **call credit spread** on the bearish (overbought) setup. Collect
  premium, defined risk = width âˆ’ credit.
- **C â€” AI entry rationale:** a 1â€“2 sentence "why we're entering and what we're watching",
  generated by Claude on each open, stored on the position and shown on the dashboard.

Carry these caveats (inherited + new):

- **The entry edge is still unproven.** A and B trade the SAME unproven signals (UNDERTOW's dip
  detector, mirrored for the bearish side) â€” expressed as debit vs credit. Paper forward-test only.
- **Fills are at mid.** Multi-leg verticals are MORE sensitive to mid-vs-real fills than single legs
  (two bid/ask spreads to cross). Paper P&L is optimistic on fills; flag this in results.
- **Overnight gap risk** is unchanged â€” multi-day holds, RTH-only monitoring.
- **Credit spreads can lose multiples of the credit** (up to width âˆ’ credit). "Juicy premium" â‰  free.
- **The AI rationale is explanatory only.** It NEVER gates, sizes, or changes a trade. It is a
  post-hoc narration of the deterministic signal. A failed/blank rationale must never block a trade.
- Paper only. Both bots ship disabled. Not financial advice.

**Sequencing:** **A â†’ C â†’ B.** A is closest to existing code; C is small and consumed by both;
B is the most net-new. UNDERTOW v1 (single calls) may stay enabled to collect entry-signal data
until A ships (the dip detector is unchanged; only the structure changes).

## 1. Shared component â€” `vertical_spread.py`

One strategy module builds any 2-leg vertical, reusing the existing executor/monitor (which already
branch credit vs debit via `CREDIT_STRATEGIES`). Four strategy keys:

| Strategy key | Direction | Type | Credit/Debit | Used by |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| `bull_call_spread` | bullish | calls | **debit** | UNDERTOW v2 |
| `bear_put_spread` | bearish | puts | **debit** | UNDERTOW v2 |
| `bull_put_spread` | bullish | puts | **credit** | DELTA |
| `bear_call_spread` | bearish | calls | **credit** | DELTA |

`bull_put_spread` and `bear_call_spread` join `CREDIT_STRATEGIES`; the two debit keys stay out (so
`compute_mtm` / `close_position` take the right P&L branch automatically).

### Leg ordering & P&L (matches existing sign convention)

`compute_mtm` signs short legs `+1`, long legs `âˆ’1` ("cost to unwind from this side"), then flips for
debit strategies. The builder must order legs accordingly and set `entry_price` to the net
debit (debit strats) or net credit (credit strats):

- **bull_call_spread** (debit): legs `[long call @K1, short call @K2>K1]`; `debit = long_mid âˆ’ short_mid`.
- **bear_put_spread** (debit): legs `[long put @K2, short put @K1<K2]`; `debit = long_mid âˆ’ short_mid`.
- **bull_put_spread** (credit): legs `[short put @K2, long put @K1<K2]`; `credit = short_mid âˆ’ long_mid`.
- **bear_call_spread** (credit): legs `[short call @K1, long call @K2>K1]`; `credit = short_mid âˆ’ long_mid`.

### Strike & width selection (greeks-free v1)

Single names span a wide price range, so strikes are chosen by **% of spot**, snapped to available
strikes â€” no greeks needed (consistent with UNDERTOW v1):

- **Debit verticals:** buy the ATM strike (nearest to spot); sell the strike ~`spread_pct` (default
  **4%**) further OTM in the trade direction.
- **Credit verticals:** sell the strike ~`short_otm_pct` (default **3%**) OTM (a ~0.20â€“0.25-delta
  proxy); buy the strike ~`spread_pct` (default **4%**) beyond that (further OTM). Width = the gap.

Delta-based strike selection is a **v2 refinement** (would require threading per-option greeks
through the chain provider). Documented, deferred.

### Signal surface

`build_vertical_signal(*, kind, chain, spot_context, config, equity, diag=None)` returns a dataclass
exposing the same surface the executor consumes: `ticker`, `expiration`, `contracts`, `legs()`,
`max_profit`, `max_loss`, `pt_target_pnl`, `sl_target_pnl`, and **either** `.debit` **or** `.credit`
(never both). Plus context fields for journaling (`direction`, `setup`, strikes, width).

- **Debit:** `max_loss = debitĂ—100`; `max_profit = (width âˆ’ debit)Ă—100`.
- **Credit:** `max_profit = creditĂ—100`; `max_loss = (width âˆ’ credit)Ă—100`.

## 2. Shared component â€” `ai_rationale.py`

A cheap, fail-safe narration of each entry. **Explanatory only â€” never gates or sizes a trade.**

- **Model:** `claude-opus-4-8` (operator choice; best writer). **Entry-only** (a handful of calls/day,
  not per-scan), `max_tokensâ‰ˆ160`, tiny structured input â†’ â‰ˆ **$1â€“2/month** total. Adaptive thinking
  off (omit `thinking`) for a terse, fast note; a final-answer-only system instruction keeps reasoning
  out of the visible text.
- **SDK:** the official `anthropic` Python SDK (add to `spreadworks/requirements.txt`). Reads
  `ANTHROPIC_API_KEY` from env.
- **Interface:** `generate_entry_rationale(*, bot, signal_context) -> str | None`.
  - `signal_context` = the structured setup already computed: ticker, direction, setup (dip/rip),
    dip/rip %, reference high/low, RSI, trend (vs 20-SMA), structure (e.g. "bull call spread"),
    strikes, width, debit/credit, max profit/loss, exit plan (PT/SL/time-stop).
  - Returns 1â€“2 sentences: *why* it entered + *what it's watching* (the level/exit that matters).
  - **Fail-safe:** any exception, timeout, missing key, or empty response â†’ return `None`. The caller
    opens the trade regardless; the rationale field is just blank.
- **Cost guardrails (hard):** entry-only; never in the monitor loop; never in a retry storm (one
  attempt, SDK default retries off for this call via `max_retries=0` + a short timeout). A module
  flag (env `SPREADWORKS_AI_RATIONALE`, default on) lets the operator kill it instantly.
- **Storage:** written into the position `notes` JSON under `"rationale"` (no schema change). Shown on
  the dashboard position card.
- **Prompt caching:** the static system prompt is < the Opus 4096-token cache minimum, so caching
  won't engage â€” keep the prompt tiny instead. No caching needed at this size/volume.

## 3. Sub-project A â€” UNDERTOW v2 (directional debit verticals)

Reuses UNDERTOW's universe, scanner universe-loop, daily-history fetch, sizing, and exit machinery.
**Replaces** the single-leg `dip_buy` structure with debit verticals; the entry *detector* is reused
and mirrored for the bearish side.

### Triggers (per ticker, per scan)

- **Bullish "dip"** (existing): price â‰¥ `dip_threshold` (3%) below the 5-day high, RSI(2) < 10
  (oversold), spot above the 20-day SMA (uptrend pullback) â†’ **bull call spread**.
- **Bearish "rip"** (new mirror): price â‰¥ `dip_threshold` (3%) above the 5-day **low**, RSI(2) > 90
  (overbought), spot **below** the 20-day SMA (downtrend bounce) â†’ **bear put spread**.
- Same universe filter (spread â‰¤ 15% of mid on each leg, leg price â‰¥ $0.20, earnings-window exclusion).
- One open position per ticker; one new open per scan (deepest dip / highest rip wins); concurrent cap.

### Contract / sizing
- Target DTE â‰ˆ 10. Buy ATM, sell ~4% OTM (the `spread_pct` knob).
- **Risk = net debit.** `contracts = floor(equity Ă— bp_pct / (debitĂ—100))`, ceiling `max_contracts`.

### Exits (debit-vertical semantics)
- **PT** = +`pt_pct` of debit (default **0.50** â†’ +50% of debit paid).
- **SL** = âˆ’`sl_pct` of debit (default **0.50** â†’ âˆ’50% of debit).
- **TIME_STOP** at `hold_days` (2). **PRE_EXPIRY** backstop. No same-day EOD.

### Migration v1 â†’ v2
UNDERTOW's registry `strategy` changes `dip_buy` â†’ a directional-vertical marker; the scanner routes
UNDERTOW to the vertical builder. Any open v1 single-call positions are still monitored by the
existing `dip_buy` exit path (kept) and close out within the 2-day time-stop; new entries are verticals.
`dip_buy.py` and its tests are retained (the detector logic is imported by the vertical path).

## 4. Sub-project B â€” DELTA (directional credit spreads)

A new sibling bot. Same universe + the SAME directional signals as UNDERTOW v2, but **sells** premium.

| Setup | Structure | Thesis |
|---|---|---|
| Bullish (oversold dip) | **sell put credit spread** below price | bounce or hold â†’ puts decay |
| Bearish (overbought rip) | **sell call credit spread** above price | rollover or stall â†’ calls decay |

- **"Juicy" filter:** `min_credit` floor (default **$0.20** net credit) + optional `min_atm_iv` floor
  (default off) so it only sells when premium is fat enough. Reject `credit_too_low` otherwise.
- **Strikes:** short ~3% OTM, long ~4% beyond (width â‰ˆ shortâ†’long gap), greeks-free.
- **Risk = width âˆ’ credit.** `contracts = floor(equity Ă— bp_pct / (max_lossĂ—100))`, ceiling.
- **Exits:** PT = +`pt_pct` of credit (default **0.50**), SL = âˆ’`sl_pct` of credit (default **1.50** â†’
  close at 150% of credit lost), TIME_STOP (2 days), PRE_EXPIRY. Credit P&L via the existing credit
  branch.
- Ships **disabled, paper-only**. Registry mirror + `/delta` dashboard.
